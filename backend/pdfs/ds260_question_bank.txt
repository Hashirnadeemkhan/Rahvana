## SECTION 1: PERSONAL, ADDRESS, AND PHONE INFORMATION

### Personal Information

**S01-Q001**

**Original question:** Name Provided (Surnames, Given Names)

**Simplified wording:** What is your full name (last name, first name)?

**Tips to answer:**
- Enter your name EXACTLY as it appears in your passport
- Use the same order as passport: surnames first, then given names
- Include all names shown; do not leave out middle names
- Common mistake: reversing surname and given name order

**Resources:**
- https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/us-visas/visa-information-resources/forms/online-immigrant-visa-forms/ds-260-faqs.html - Official DS-260 FAQs

---

**S01-Q002**

**Original question:** Full Name in Native Language

**Simplified wording:** What is your name written in your native alphabet (if not Roman letters)?

**Tips to answer:**
- Only required if your native language uses non-Roman characters (Arabic, Chinese, Russian, etc.)
- Type your name in the native script
- If your native language uses Roman letters (English, Spanish, French), you may skip this or write "SAME"

---

**S01-Q003**

**Original question:** Other Names Used

**Simplified wording:** Have you ever used any other names?

**Tips to answer:**
- Include maiden names, previous married names, nicknames used on official documents
- Include aliases, professional names, religious names if used formally
- Do NOT include casual nicknames never used on documents
- Answer "NO" only if you have never used another name officially

---

**S01-Q004**

**Original question:** Sex

**Simplified wording:** What is your sex/gender?

**Tips to answer:**
- Select Male or Female as shown on your passport
- Must match your passport exactly
- If passport shows different gender from birth certificate, use passport

---

**S01-Q005**

**Original question:** Current Marital Status

**Simplified wording:** What is your marital status right now?

**Tips to answer:**
- Options: Single, Married, Widowed, Divorced, Legally Separated
- Choose current status on the date you fill out the form
- If divorced, you must have final divorce decree
- If separated but not legally divorced, choose "Married"

**Resources:**
- You will need civil documents (marriage certificate, divorce decree, death certificate) to prove status

---

**S01-Q006**

**Original question:** Date of Birth

**Simplified wording:** What is your date of birth?

**Tips to answer:**
- Use format: DD Month YYYY (e.g., 15 January 1985)
- Must match your passport and birth certificate
- If documents show different dates, bring both to interview and explain
- Cannot be changed after submission without unlocking form

**Resources:**
- If birth certificate unavailable, check https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/us-visas/visa-information-resources/reciprocity-country-documents.html for alternatives

---

**S01-Q007**

**Original question:** City of Birth

**Simplified wording:** In what city or town were you born?

**Tips to answer:**
- Use the city name as shown on your birth certificate
- If born in rural area, use nearest town or district
- Spell using Roman letters

---

**S01-Q008**

**Original question:** State/Province of Birth

**Simplified wording:** In what state, province, or region were you born?

**Tips to answer:**
- Use official state/province name from birth certificate
- If country has no states/provinces, enter "DOES NOT APPLY" or the country name
- For former countries (e.g., USSR, Yugoslavia), use the region at time of birth

---

**S01-Q009**

**Original question:** Country/Region of Birth

**Simplified wording:** In what country were you born?

**Tips to answer:**
- Use current country name (e.g., if born in USSR, specify Russia, Ukraine, etc.)
- If country no longer exists, use modern equivalent
- Must match birth certificate country field

---

**S01-Q010**

**Original question:** Country/Region of Origin (Nationality)

**Simplified wording:** What is your current nationality/citizenship?

**Tips to answer:**
- Enter the country that issued your passport
- If you have dual citizenship, enter the nationality of the passport you will use
- This is your citizenship, not your ethnicity or place of birth

**Resources:**
- You will be asked about other nationalities in a follow-up question

### Address and Phone Information

**S01-Q017**

**Original question:** Present Address (Street Address, City, State/Province, Postal Code, Country, From Date)

**Simplified wording:** What is your current home address and how long have you lived there?

**Tips to answer:**
- Provide your physical address where you currently live
- Include apartment/unit number if applicable
- From Date: month and year you moved to this address (e.g., "JANUARY 2020")
- This must be your actual residence, not a P.O. Box

**Resources:**
- Keep utility bills or lease agreements as proof of address

---

**S01-Q018**

**Original question:** Have you lived anywhere other than this address since the age of sixteen?

**Simplified wording:** Have you lived at any other addresses since you turned 16 years old?

**Tips to answer:**
- Answer YES if you have lived anywhere else since age 16
- You must list ALL addresses for the years since age 16
- Include addresses in all countries
- Prepare list in advance: address, city, country, from/to dates
- List in reverse chronological order (most recent first)

**Resources:**
- Gather old leases, school records, or employer records to help remember addresses and dates

---

**S01-Q019**

**Original question:** Primary Phone Number

**Simplified wording:** What is your main phone number?

**Tips to answer:**
- Include country code (e.g., +92 for Pakistan)
- Format: +[country code][number] or as CEAC format allows
- Must be a number where you can be reached
- NVC and embassy will use this to contact you

## SECTION 3: PREVIOUS U.S. TRAVEL INFORMATION

**S03-Q001**

**Original question:** Have you ever been in the U.S.?

**Simplified wording:** Have you ever visited or lived in the United States?

**Tips to answer:**
- Answer YES if you have EVER been to the U.S., even for one day
- Includes tourist visits, business trips, student visas, transit
- If YES, you must provide details of last 5 visits
- Common mistake: forgetting short visits or airport transits

**Resources:**
- Check passport stamps for exact dates if unsure

---

**S03-Q002**

**Original question:** Were you issued an Alien Registration Number by the Department of Homeland Security?

**Simplified wording:** Did U.S. immigration ever give you an A-Number?

**Tips to answer:**
- A-Number is an 8-9 digit number (e.g., A012345678)
- You have one if you previously applied for green card, asylum, or certain work permits
- Check old immigration documents, work permits (EAD), or notices
- Answer NO if you only had tourist visas

**Resources:**
- https://www.uscis.gov/i9-central/form-i-9-resources/handbook-for-employers-m-274/120-acceptable-documents-for-verifying-employment-authorization-and-identity/121-list-a-documents-that-establish-both-identity-and-employment-authorization - Where to find A-Number

---

**S03-Q003**

**Original question:** Provide information on your last five U.S. visits (Date Arrived, Length of Stay)

**Simplified wording:** When did you visit the U.S. in the past and how long did you stay each time?

**Tips to answer:**
- List up to 5 most recent U.S. visits
- For each visit provide: arrival date and length of stay (days/months)
- Check passport stamps for exact dates
- Include purpose of visit if asked (tourism, business, family, etc.)

**Resources:**
- I-94 travel history available at https://i94.cbp.dhs.gov/I94/#/history-search

---

**S03-Q004**

**Original question:** Have you ever been issued a U.S. Visa?

**Simplified wording:** Have you ever received a visa to enter the United States?

**Tips to answer:**
- Answer YES if you ever had any type of U.S. visa (tourist, student, work, etc.)
- Check old passports for visa stamps or stickers
- If YES, provide details: issue date, visa type, visa number

---

**S03-Q005**

**Original question:** Date Visa Was Issued

**Simplified wording:** When was your U.S. visa issued?

**Tips to answer:**
- Date shown on visa stamp/sticker
- Format: DD Month YYYY
- Provide for most recent visa if you had multiple

---

**S03-Q006**

**Original question:** Visa Classification

**Simplified wording:** What type of visa did you have?

**Tips to answer:**
- Common types: B1/B2 (tourist/business), F1 (student), H1B (work), J1 (exchange)
- Found on visa stamp
- Be specific (e.g., "B1/B2" not just "tourist")

**Resources:**
- https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/us-visas/visa-information-resources/all-visa-categories.html - Visa types reference

**Tips to answer:**

---

**S03-Q008**

**Original question:** Have any of your U.S. visas ever been lost or stolen?

**Simplified wording:** Has any U.S. visa you received ever been lost or stolen?

**Tips to answer:**
- Answer YES if a visa in an old passport was lost/stolen
- Provide explanation if YES

---

**S03-Q009**

**Original question:** Have any of your U.S. visas ever been cancelled or revoked?

**Simplified wording:** Has the U.S. government ever canceled a visa you had?

**Tips to answer:**
- Answer YES if embassy ever cancelled your visa
- Includes visas marked "cancelled without prejudice"

---

**S03-Q010**

**Original question:** Have you ever been refused a U.S. visa, been refused admission to the U.S., or withdrawn your application for admission at the port of entry?

**Simplified wording:** Have you ever been denied a U.S. visa or been refused entry to the U.S.?

**Tips to answer:**
- Answer YES if embassy ever denied your visa application

**Tips to answer:**
- Enter your current job title or occupation

- Be specific (e.g., "Software Engineer" not just "Engineer")
- Use standard occupation names when possible

---

**S04-Q002**

**Original question:** Do you have any other occupations?

**Simplified wording:** Do you have any additional jobs?

**Tips to answer:**
- Answer YES if you have part-time or secondary employment

**Simplified wording:** What type of work do you plan to do in the United States?

**Tips to answer:**

---

**S04-Q004**

**Original question:** Were you previously employed?

**Simplified wording:** Have you had jobs in the past?

---

## SECTION 6: SECURITY AND BACKGROUND INFORMATION

**IMPORTANT:** All Security and Background questions must be answered truthfully. False answers can result in permanent visa ineligibility and criminal charges. If you must answer YES to any question, provide complete details and consult an immigration attorney.

### Health-Related Questions

**S06-Q001**

**Original question:** Do you have a communicable disease of public health significance such as tuberculosis (TB)?

**Simplified wording:** Do you have tuberculosis (TB) or another contagious disease that is a public health concern?

**Tips to answer:**
- Answer based on current health status
- TB is most common issue - you will have medical exam before interview
- Answer NO unless diagnosed currently
- Medical exam at interview will screen for these diseases
- Having had TB in the past (and cured) is usually not a problem

**Resources:**
- https://www.cdc.gov/immigrantrefugeehealth/panel-physicians/panel-physicians.html - List of approved doctors for medical exam

---

**S06-Q002**

**Original question:** Do you have documentation to establish that you have received vaccinations in accordance with U.S. law?

**Simplified wording:** Do you have proof that you received all required vaccinations?

**Tips to answer:**
- Answer YES if you have vaccination records
- Required vaccines: MMR, Tdap, Varicella, Influenza, Polio, Hepatitis A&B, COVID-19, others
- Panel physician will review/update vaccines at medical exam
- Bring any vaccination records you have to medical exam
- Most applicants answer YES - doctor will help complete any missing vaccines

**Resources:**
- https://www.cdc.gov/immigrantrefugeehealth/panel-physicians/vaccinations.html - Vaccination requirements

---

**S06-Q003**

**Original question:** Do you have a mental or physical disorder that poses or is likely to pose a threat to the safety or welfare of yourself or others?

**Simplified wording:** Do you have a mental or physical condition that could make you dangerous to yourself or others?

**Tips to answer:**
- Answer NO unless you have serious untreated mental illness with history of violence
- Having depression, anxiety, or physical disability is NOT a "YES" answer
- Only answer YES if condition involves danger/threat of harm
- Panel physician will evaluate during medical exam
- Seek legal advice if unsure

---

**S06-Q004**

**Original question:** Are you or have you ever been a drug abuser or addict?

**Simplified wording:** Are you currently or have you ever been addicted to drugs?

**Tips to answer:**
- Answer truthfully - includes illegal drugs and misuse of prescription drugs
- Past drug use without addiction: generally answer NO
- Current or past addiction: answer YES and explain
- Rehabilitation and time passed can help overcome this issue
- Consult attorney if YES - may need waiver

### Criminal History Questions

**S06-Q005**

**Original question:** Have you ever been arrested or convicted for any offense or crime, even though subject of a pardon, amnesty, or other similar action?

- Obtain police certificates and court documents
- Consult attorney if YES - criminal history can lead to inadmissibility

### Important Reminders

- **Answer ALL questions truthfully.** Fraud or misrepresentation can result in permanent visa ineligibility.
- **The DS-260 can be saved and resumed later.** You do NOT need to complete it in one sitting.
- **After submitting, you can unlock the form to make changes** before your interview.
- **Bring ALL civil documents to your interview:** birth certificate, marriage certificate, divorce decrees, police certificates, etc.
- **If you answer YES to any Security and Background question** (especially terrorism, serious crimes, or immigration fraud), consult an immigration attorney BEFORE submitting.
- **Keep copies of everything you submit.**

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Typos in names, dates, or passport numbers
- Inconsistent information between DS-260 and supporting documents
- Forgetting to list previous addresses, employers, or travel history
- Not disclosing previous visa denials or arrests
- Leaving sections blank instead of entering "DOES NOT APPLY" or "NONE"
- Not reviewing social media accounts before disclosure
